    FrontierCRM — User Personas & Feature Consolidation

    Overview: 8 user personas derived from competitive research (T1: market/competitive analysis of 10 CRM vendors) and feature analysis (T2: deep-dive on Salesforce Sales Cloud). Each persona maps to the features FrontierCRM must support to win the SMB 10-200 seat sweet spot.

    PART 1: USER PERSONAS

    Persona 1: Alex — SMB Sales Rep

    Role: Sales Development Rep (SDR) / Account Executive at a 10-200 person company
    Age: 24-35 | Tech: High — Slack, Gmail/Outlook, Zoom, Notion
    Decision Role: User only | CRM Experience: May have used Salesforce/HubSpot/Pipedrive

    Goals

    • Hit quota consistently. Every CRM hour is not selling.
    • Log calls, emails, meetings effortlessly — auto when possible.
    • See next-best-action per deal.
    • Full mobile access.

    Pain Points

    • Data entry = tax on selling time. Manual logging gets skipped.
    • Hard to find last touchpoint quickly — wastes 5-10 min/call prep.
    • Mobile apps are stripped-down.
    • Complex CRMs need too many clicks.
    • Manager chases them to update pipeline.

    What They Need

    • One-click activity capture, lightning-fast pipeline updates, full mobile parity, AI next-best-action, clean beautiful UI.

    Persona 2: Jordan — Enterprise Sales Rep

    Role: Enterprise Account Executive at 500+ person company
    Age: 30-45 | Tech: Moderate — Salesforce daily, Excel power user
    Decision Role: Power user | CRM Experience: 5+ years Salesforce

    Goals

    • Manage complex multi-stakeholder deals (8-20 touchpoints) with clear visibility
    • Track POCs, evaluations, procurement cycles
    • Collaborate with SEs, CS, management on deal strategy
    • Accurate forecasting for comp planning

    Pain Points

    • Salesforce is slow and bloated — too many fields, too many clicks
    • Multi-threaded deal tracking is manual (stakeholder maps in spreadsheets)
    • Forecast call prep is painful — exporting data, reconciling with gut feel
    • Can't easily see what SE/CSM did with the account recently
    • Territory changes break historical pipeline visibility

    What They Need

    • Multi-threaded deal tracking with stakeholder maps, team activity feed, AI forecasting, fast performance, AI-summarized notes

    Persona 3: Morgan — Sales Manager

    Role: Sales Manager at 10-200 person company. Manages 5-15 reps.
    Age: 30-45 | Tech: Moderate — reporting tools, not admin-level
    Decision Role: Recommends tools, strong influence | CRM Experience: 1-2 CRMs

    Goals

    • Real-time pipeline health without hunting spreadsheets
    • Identify at-risk deals — coach before they slip
    • Understand why deals stall (stage duration, activity gaps)
    • Run accurate weekly forecast calls without chasing reps
    • Coach reps on specific deals

    Pain Points

    • Pipeline reports are 2-3 days stale
    • CRMs surface raw data, not insights — analysis is manual
    • Can't easily see rep activity vs results
    • Forecast calls devolve into "trust me" without CRM data
    • No easy way to spot coaching opportunities

    What They Need

    • Real-time pipeline dashboard, deal velocity analysis, rep activity analytics, AI deal risk scoring, manager dashboard with drill-down, forecast accuracy tracking

    Persona 4: Riley — Sales Director / VP of Sales

    Role: Sales Director / VP at 50-200 person company
    Age: 35-55 | Tech: Low-medium — email, calendar, dashboards
    Decision Role: Primary buyer, controls budget up to $50K/yr | CRM Experience: 3-5 CRMs

    Goals

    • Accurate revenue forecasting to board/investors
    • Territory planning — coverage without overlap
    • Identify systemic pipeline problems
    • Measure rep and team performance objectively
    • Scale the sales org with onboarding and playbooks

    Pain Points

    • Forecasting is a black art — CRM data always wrong, adjustments manual
    • Territory disputes — no single source of truth for assignment
    • No visibility into sales process adherence
    • Onboarding new reps takes 4-8 weeks to CRM proficiency
    • Can't get reliable answers without analyst time
    • CRM buying feels like a hostage situation

    What They Need

    • Executive dashboard (revenue, pipeline coverage, forecast accuracy), territory management, sales process analytics, playbook support, multi-period forecasting, predictable pricing

    Persona 5: Casey — CRM Admin / IT Manager

    Role: CRM Admin / IT Manager at 50-500 person company
    Age: 28-50 | Tech: Very high — scripting, Flow, APIs
    Decision Role: Technical evaluator, strong veto | CRM Experience: Lives in CRM daily

    Goals

    • Configure CRM to match business processes without code
    • Maintain data quality — dedup, import/export, validation
    • Integrate CRM with tech stack (ERP, billing, marketing)
    • Manage user access, roles, permissions cleanly
    • Train new users and maintain adoption

    Pain Points

    • Apex/Deluge = proprietary lock-in, every customization is tech debt
    • Simple changes require release cycles and admin approval
    • Data import/export is harder than it should be
    • Integrations break on CRM updates
    • Managing 200-500 users = provisioning overhead
    • Platform upgrades force retesting all customizations

    What They Need

    • No-code configuration, API-first with webhooks, bulk import/export, RBAC, backward-compatible releases, integration marketplace, audit log

    Persona 6: Sam — Small Business Owner

    Role: Founder / Owner / CEO of 3-15 person business (micro-SMB)
    Age: 30-55 | Tech: Low-medium — email, calendar, QuickBooks
    Decision Role: Sole decision-maker, signs the check | CRM Experience: Tried free CRM, went back to spreadsheets

    Goals

    • See all customer interactions in one place
    • Never miss follow-ups — automated reminders
    • Minimal setup — useful day 1
    • One predictable monthly cost
    • Focus on running the business, not adminning CRM

    Pain Points

    • Spreadsheets don't scale but real CRMs are too complex
    • Can't afford a CRM consultant or dedicated admin
    • HubSpot free tier limited; paid tiers expensive for small team
    • No time to train employees on complex system
    • Needs sales + support + billing in one tool
    • Fear of data lock-in

    What They Need

    • Minimal setup (10 minutes), all-in-one dashboard (leads, deals, tickets, tasks), flat pricing, email + calendar sync out of box, easy data export, mobile-first

    Persona 7: Taylor — Customer Support Agent

    Role: Customer Support / Success Agent at 50-500 person company
    Age: 22-40 | Tech: Moderate — ticketing, chat, email
    Decision Role: End user, provides feedback | CRM Experience: Support modules (Service Cloud, Zendesk)

    Goals

    • See full customer history when a ticket comes in (sales context, past tickets, deals)
    • Resolve issues quickly without searching multiple systems
    • Log interactions without disrupting workflow
    • Escalate to sales when support issue has revenue implications
    • Track SLAs and response times

    Pain Points

    • Support and sales data are siloed
    • No visibility into whether customer is in procurement
    • Can't see if customer just got upsold before a support call
    • Ticket routing ignores relationship history
    • No centralized knowledge base integrated with CRM
    • Escalation means leaving CRM to Slack

    What They Need

    • Customer 360 (sales + support unified), ticket management with SLAs, shared context with sales, collision detection, integrated knowledge base, collaboration tools

    Persona 8: Dana — C-Suite Executive (CEO / CRO / COO)

    Role: CEO / CRO / COO at 50-500 person company
    Age: 40-65 | Tech: Low — email, iPhone, doesn't touch CRM directly
    Decision Role: Ultimate buyer, signs contract, ROI-focused | CRM Experience: Pitched by every vendor

    Goals

    • Revenue predictability — know next quarter with confidence
    • Team productivity — are they selling or adminning?
    • Customer retention — at-a-glance churn risk, health scores
    • Operational efficiency — reduce tool bloat, consolidate vendors
    • ROI on tool investments

    Pain Points

    • 5+ dashboards (CRM, BI, spreadsheets) — none agree
    • Forecasts consistently wrong — always surprised by misses
    • No clear view of team performance
    • CRM vendor relationships adversarial — locked in, hidden price increases
    • No straight answer on tool consolidation
    • Customer churn visible only after the fact — no early warning

    What They Need

    • Executive dashboard (ARR, pipeline coverage, NRR, churn), customer health scoring with AI risk flags, revenue forecasting with confidence intervals, team performance KPIs, vendor consolidation, transparent reporting, predictable total cost

    PART 4: PERSONA COVERAGE & CONSOLIDATION ANALYSIS

    Persona Clusters

    The 8 personas naturally cluster into 4 functional groups that share feature needs:

    Cluster Personas Shared P0 Features Design Implication
    Frontline Sellers Alex (SMB Rep), Jordan (Ent Rep), Sam (Biz Owner) Pipeline, Email sync, Auto-logging, Mobile, AI next-best-action Fast, low-click UI. Mobile parity. Zero data entry friction.
    Sales Leadership Morgan (Mgr), Riley (Dir/VP), Dana (Exec) Dashboards, Forecasting, Activity analytics, Deal velocity Insight-forward design. Dashboards, not data entry. Executive summary layer.
    Operations & Admin Casey (Admin) No-code config, API, RBAC, Import/export, Audit, Integration Power tools. Not default UI — accessed via settings/workspace. Backward compatibility.
    Customer Facing Taylor (Support), Sam (Biz Owner as support handler) Ticket management, Customer 360, Knowledge base, SLA Unified sales + support view. Collision detection.

    Key Consolidation Decisions

    Based on cross-persona analysis:

    1. Email sync is the single highest-ROI engineering investment. Every selling persona rates it P0. Salesforce's weakness here is FrontierCRM's wedge opportunity.

    2. Mobile is not optional — it's a fundamental requirement. 5 of 8 personas rate it P0. Must deliver full feature parity, not a trimmed-down companion.

    3. AI must be included, not upsold. Lead scoring, next-best-action, meeting summaries, and email drafting all score 12+. Gating these behind premium tiers contradicts the transparent-positioning strategy from T1.

    4. Support/Service features should ship in the same product, not as a separate SKU. The all-in-one positioning is a market gap. Taylor and Sam both need customer 360 with ticket management.

    5. Admin tools should be powerful but invisible. Casey (Admin) has P0 needs that no other persona cares about. Build a dedicated admin workspace rather than cluttering the main UI.

    6. Territory management and comp planning are Phase 2. Only Riley and Dana need these. Don't build before core pipeline and reporting are solid.

    7. Backward-compatible releases are non-negotiable for admin adoption. Casey will block adoption if forced migrations are a concern. This is FrontierCRM's answer to Salesforce's #3 user complaint — forced feature retirement and breaking changes.

    Summary for Engineering

    Priority Tier Features Timeframe Engineering Strategy
    Tier 1: Core (16+ score) 8 features: pipeline, email sync, auto-logging, activity history, calendar sync, dashboards, contact view, mobile app Ship first Build these rock-solid. No shortcuts. These are the table stakes.
    Tier 2: Differentiator (12-15) 16 features: AI scoring, AI summaries, AI next-best-action, collaboration, workflows, Slack, analytics, push, reporting, email drafting, deal velocity, multi-stage, tickets, customer 360, KB, internal notes Ship second These are FrontierCRM's moat. Invest in quality — they're why someone switches.
    Tier 3: Parity (8-11) 20 features: duplicates, forecasting, templates, offline, lists, exec dashboard, stakeholder maps, import/export, RBAC, predictive, lead routing, deal rooms, approvals, auto-tagging, health scoring, field builder, email tracking, enrichment, portal, file sharing First 6 months Match competitor baseline. Every major CRM has these; FrontierCRM needs to match.
    Tier 4: Phase 2 (< 8) 6 features: ERP integrations, scheduling, API, e-commerce 6-12 months Expand into vertical needs after core is solid.
