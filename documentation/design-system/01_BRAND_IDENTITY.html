    FrontierCRM — Brand Identity & Guidelines

    Version: 1.0 Date: 2026-06-28 Status: Final Draft Derived from: User Personas (T5), UX Workflows (T6), Technical Architecture (T4)

    1. Brand Foundation

    1.1 Brand Name

    FrontierCRM

    No acronym. No dot-anything suffix. The name combines "frontier" — the edge of what's possible, new territory for growing businesses — with "CRM" as the category signal. It positions the product as a modern, forward-looking alternative to Salesforce (the incumbent) and HubSpot (the friendly generalist).

    1.2 Brand Tagline

    "Your sales. Your way."

    Reinforces flexibility, configurability, and empowerment. Speaks to SMBs who outgrew spreadsheets and enterprises tired of one-size-fits-all CRM dogma.

    1.3 Brand Personality

    Five traits, ordered by priority:

    # Trait Means Doesn't Mean
    1 Trustworthy Secure, reliable, handles your customer data with care. Certifications, uptime SLAs, transparent pricing. Boring, safe, slow-moving
    2 Modern Current design language, fast interactions, AI-native, mobile-first. Ships quarterly, not annually. Trendy, unstable, unfamiliar
    3 Efficient Gets you to the action in 1-2 clicks. Defaults are smart. The system does data entry so you can sell. Minimalist to the point of missing features
    4 Light Fast load times, uncluttered UI, progressive disclosure. Works on a 4-year-old laptop and a phone. Shallow, lacking depth
    5 Unique Doesn't look or feel like Salesforce v2. Own design language, original interactions, distinct voice. Different for difference's sake

    1.4 Brand Archetype

    The Explorer — with a strong dose of The Sage and The Magician.

    Explorer: "There's always new territory." Growth, discovery, pushing boundaries.
    Sage: "Here's the data." Transparency, intelligence, wisdom over hype.
    Magician: "Let me handle the tedious stuff." Automation, AI, transformation.

    1.5 Competitive Positioning

                Trustworthy
                │
          Salesforce ●
                │
  Simple ───────┼─────── Complex
                │
     ● Pipedrive│
                │     ● HubSpot
          ● Frontier

          (the new CRM)
              Modern

    FrontierCRM sits at the intersection of trustworthy + modern, with a bias toward simple over complex — the gap no incumbent fills well.

    2. Tone of Voice

    2.1 Principles

    Principle In Practice
    Helpful Anticipate needs. "Here are the 3 leads that need attention today."
    Direct Say what's happening. No fluff. "Your import finished. 47 contacts added."
    Confident Know the product. "You can set this up in 2 minutes."

    2.2 Voice Spectrum

    Context Tone Example
    Error message Direct + helpful "We couldn't send that email. The recipient's server rejected it. [Check domain settings]"
    Empty state Encouraging + clear "No deals yet. Ready to add your first one?"
    Success Confident + concise "Deal closed. Moving to won."
    Onboarding Warm + instructive "Let's get your pipeline set up. It takes about 3 minutes."
    System notification Neutral + informative "Sync complete. 12 emails imported from your inbox."
    AI suggestion Curious + confident "Based on recent activity, this deal might need attention."
    Upgrade/pricing Direct + transparent "You're on the Growth plan. Here's what you'd get on Scale."

    2.3 Words to Use and Avoid

    Prefer: - "You can..." (empowering) over "The system allows..." - "Set up" over "Configure" - "Pipeline" over "Sales funnel" (more concrete) - "Team" over "Organization" or "Tenant" - "Activity" over "Engagement" - "Let's" in onboarding, "Here's" in notifications

    Avoid: - "Revolutionize", "Game-changing", "Next-gen", "Best-in-class" - "Leverage", "Synergize", "Holistic" - "User" when referring to people (use "you" or "your team") - "SaaS" in customer-facing copy (it's assumed) - Acronyms without explanation (DNC, MQL, SQL spelled out first time)

    2.4 Writing for the Product

    Button labels: Verb-first. "Add contact", "Send email", "Merge deals". Never "OK".

    Empty states: State + next action. "No contacts in this list. [Import from CSV] or [Add one manually]."

    Notifications: Subject line format: [Context] + [Outcome] + [Optional action].
    Example: "Lead assignment: 14 leads routed to Alex Chen. [View in pipeline]"

    Error messages: What happened + why + what to do.
    Example: "Email send failed (bounced — mailbox full). Recipient notified. [Remove from list]"

    4. Typography

    4.1 Typeface Selection

    Role Typeface Fallback Stack
    Headings Inter (sans-serif) Inter, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif
    Body Inter (sans-serif) Same as above
    Code/Monospace JetBrains Mono 'JetBrains Mono', 'Fira Code', 'SF Mono', 'Cascadia Code', Consolas, monospace

    Rationale for Inter: Excellent screen rendering at all sizes. Neutral but warm character shapes. Variable font gives us weight range 300-700 without loading multiple files. Open source (Rasmus Andersson, SIL OFL).

    Rationale for JetBrains Mono: Highly legible with distinctive glyphs. Ligatures optional (developer preference). Open source.

    4.2 Type Scale

    Level Size Weight Line Height Letter Spacing Usage
    Display 36px / 2.25rem 700 (Bold) 1.2 -0.02em Hero text, empty states, onboarding titles
    Heading 1 28px / 1.75rem 700 (Bold) 1.25 -0.015em Page titles, modal headers
    Heading 2 22px / 1.375rem 600 (Semi-bold) 1.3 -0.01em Section headers, sidebar section titles
    Heading 3 18px / 1.125rem 600 (Semi-bold) 1.35 -0.005em Card titles, panel headers, form group labels
    Heading 4 16px / 1rem 600 (Semi-bold) 1.4 0 Subsection titles, kanban column headers
    Body Large 16px / 1rem 400 (Regular) 1.5 0 Primary body text, table cell content
    Body 14px / 0.875rem 400 (Regular) 1.5 0 Default body text, form labels, nav items
    Body Small 13px / 0.8125rem 400 (Regular) 1.4 0 Metadata, descriptions, helper text
    Caption 12px / 0.75rem 400 (Regular) 1.33 0.01em Timestamps, badges, legal text
    Caption Bold 12px / 0.75rem 600 (Semi-bold) 1.33 0.01em Status labels, data viz axis labels
    Code 13px / 0.8125rem 400 (Regular) 1.5 0 Inline code, code blocks, field keys

    4.3 Responsive Type Scaling

    Viewport Scale Factor Notes
    ≥1200px (desktop) 1× Full type scale as above
    768-1199px (tablet) 0.95× Reduce H1/H2 by 2px, keep body same
    <768px (mobile) 0.9× All headings down one step. Body stays 14px minimum for readability

    4.4 Font Loading Strategy

    • Inter: @fontsource/inter npm package or Google Fonts with display=swap
    • JetBrains Mono: @fontsource/jetbrains-mono
    • Preload both on first paint via <link rel="preload">
    • System font fallback renders immediately while fonts load (font-display: swap)
    • Use font-variation-settings for weight axis in Inter variable font

    5. Logo & Brand Mark

    5.1 Concept Direction

    The FrontierCRM logo mark is a compass-forward arrow — a compass rose with the north point extended into a forward-pointing arrow. It symbolizes:

    • Direction — knowing where you're heading (pipeline, forecasting)
    • Exploration — new territory, growth, opportunity
    • Forward motion — progress, not just tracking

    5.2 Logo Variations

    Variant Description Primary Use
    Full lockup Mark + "FrontierCRM" wordmark in Inter Bold Website headers, product login screen, printed materials
    Mark only The compass-forward arrow alone Favicon, app icon, mobile home screen, avatar placeholder
    Wordmark only "FrontierCRM" in Inter Bold with the blue-teal gradient Navigation header (space-constrained), email signatures

    5.3 Logo Usage Rules

    • Clear space: Minimum of one "F" character width on all sides
    • Minimum size: Mark — 24px. Full lockup — 80px wide
    • Forbidden: Recolor the mark outside brand colors. Apply effects (drop shadows, gradients within the mark, bevels). Rotate the arrow. Place on low-contrast backgrounds (ratio < 3:1 with the mark)
    • Dark background: Use the white version of the mark
    • Light background: Use the blue version of the mark

    5.4 App Icon

    The app icon (PWA, mobile, desktop) is the mark set in a rounded square with a gradient background (primary-500 → secondary-500, 135°). Single-color white mark centered at 40% of the icon size.

    6. Imagery & Iconography

    6.1 Illustration Style

    Clean, minimal line art with selective color fills. Flat illustrations with subtle depth (one shadow layer max). Color palette limited to brand colors. No photos of people in product UI — use abstract illustrations representing concepts (pipeline as flowing lines, data as organized dots, communication as connected nodes).

    6.2 Icon Library

    Lucide (open source, MIT licensed) as the primary icon set.

    • Clean geometric style consistent with brand
    • All icons at 16px (inline), 20px (menu), 24px (page level)
    • Only brand-color fills for active states, neutral-400 for default states
    • No duotone, no outlined + filled mixing

    6.3 Photography (Marketing Site)

    Photo style: Authentic, candid workspace shots from real sales environments. Not staged stock-photo-smiling-at-laptops. Warm color grading pulled toward amber. Person-facing shots preferred over screen-facing.

    7. Spacing & Layout Rhythm

    7.1 Base Unit

    4px base unit. All spacing, padding, margins, and grid gaps are multiples of 4px.

    Token Rem/Ems Pixels Usage
    space-0 0 0 No space
    space-1 0.25rem 4px Inner padding in tight components
    space-2 0.5rem 8px Stacked elements, icon spacing
    space-3 0.75rem 12px Button padding, tight card padding
    space-4 1rem 16px Standard padding, card padding
    space-5 1.25rem 20px Form field padding
    space-6 1.5rem 24px Section spacing, modal padding
    space-8 2rem 32px Large section spacing
    space-10 2.5rem 40px Page section gaps
    space-12 3rem 48px Page margins
    space-16 4rem 64px Major page sections

    7.2 Content Max Width

    • Narrow content (forms, modals, wizards): 480px / 30rem
    • Standard content (detail views, settings): 720px / 45rem
    • Wide content (pipeline kanban, tables): 100% minus sidebar — max 1440px / 90rem

    8. Motion & Animation

    8.1 Duration Tokens

    Token Duration Usage
    duration-100 100ms Micro-interactions (button press, checkbox toggle)
    duration-200 200ms Standard transitions (hover, focus, panel slide)
    duration-300 300ms Modal open/close, drawer slide
    duration-400 400ms Page transitions, notifications enter/exit
    duration-500 500ms Loading skeleton fade

    8.2 Easing Curves

    Token Curve Usage
    ease-out cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1) UI entering (panels, modals, tooltips) — fast start, decelerate
    ease-in-out cubic-bezier(0.65, 0, 0.35, 1) Hover/focus transitions, color changes
    ease-spring cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1) Like, drag-to-reorder, micro-delight

    8.3 Animation Principles

    • Motion must have a purpose — never animate for decoration
    • Duration scales with distance: small elements move fast, large elements move slow
    • Elements entering the screen accelerate out; elements leaving decelerate in
    • Reduce motion: respect prefers-reduced-motion. Substitute opacity transitions for translate transitions
    • No bouncing, no parallax, no confetti without user action

    9. Accessibility (Brand-Level)

    All brand decisions are made with WCAG 2.1 AA as a minimum:

    • Color contrast: All functional text combinations verified against both light and dark backgrounds. Primary text on white = 6.1:1 (exceeds AA's 4.5:1)
    • Interactive targets: Minimum 44×44px touch target for all interactive elements (buttons, toggles, icon buttons)
    • Focus indicators: 3px brand-500 outline with 2px white offset ring — visible on all backgrounds
    • Text resizing: All type in relative units (rem). Layout withstands 200% zoom without horizontal scroll
    • Reduced motion: All brand animations degrade gracefully. Information never conveyed through motion alone

    10. Brand Application Examples

    10.1 Product UI

    The branding lives primarily in the product. The brand color appears in: - Navigation active states (primary-500 left border + text color) - Primary CTA buttons (primary-500 background → primary-600 hover) - Link text (primary-600 → primary-700 hover, with underline on hover) - Loading indicators (primary-500 spinner) - Notification badges (primary-500 dot)

    10.2 Marketing Site

    Extended typography (Inter at full weight range). Photography above the fold. Illustration style in feature sections. Blue+teal gradient hero sections with white text overlay.

    10.3 Email Communications

    Transactional: Plain, minimal, text-first with brand signature block. Marketing: One brand-color accent bar at top, minimal logo, body in Inter. Both render well without images.
