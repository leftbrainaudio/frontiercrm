    FrontierCRM — User Personas & Feature Consolidation

    Overview: 8 user personas derived from competitive research (T1: market/competitive analysis of 10 CRM vendors) and feature analysis (T2: deep-dive on Salesforce Sales Cloud). Each persona maps to the features FrontierCRM must support to win the SMB 10-200 seat sweet spot.

    PART 1: USER PERSONAS

    Persona 1: Alex — SMB Sales Rep

    Attribute Detail
    Role Sales Development Rep (SDR) / Account Executive at a 10-200 person company
    Age 24-35
    Tech Proficiency High — uses Slack, Gmail/Outlook, Zoom, Notion daily. Comfortable with new tools.
    Decision-Making Role User only. Recommends tools but does not approve budget.
    CRM Experience May have used Salesforce, HubSpot, or Pipedrive at a previous company. Currently may be using spreadsheets + email.

    Goals

    • Hit quota consistently. Every hour spent in the CRM is an hour not spent selling.
    • Log calls, emails, and meetings effortlessly — zero friction, ideally automatic.
    • See next-best-action for each deal without hunting through notes.
    • Access full CRM from phone between meetings, at trade shows, on the go.

    Pain Points

    • Data entry is a tax on selling time. Manual logging = skipped logging = inaccurate pipeline.
    • CRMs make it hard to find the last touchpoint quickly. Wastes 5-10 minutes per call prep.
    • Mobile apps are stripped-down — can't send an email or update a stage from the phone.
    • Complex CRMs (Salesforce) require too many clicks to do simple things.
    • Manager chases them to update pipeline before weekly forecast calls.

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Check email, review calendar, open CRM to see today's meetings and their deal context
    2. Log calls throughout the day (dials + scheduled)
    3. Send follow-up emails from CRM or from gmail (needs to auto-log)
    4. Update deal stages after discovery calls or demos
    5. End of day: Quick check of tomorrow's schedule
    6. Weekly: Update pipeline, move stalled deals, add notes

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • One-click activity capture — calls, emails, meetings logged automatically
    • Lightning-fast pipeline — update stage in 1-2 clicks from any screen
    • Full mobile parity — send emails, update deals, view contacts from phone
    • Next-best-action — AI surfaces the highest-impact activity for each deal
    • Clean beautiful UI — the CRM should feel good to use, not like a chore

    Persona 2: Jordan — Enterprise Sales Rep

    Attribute Detail
    Role Enterprise Account Executive at 500+ person company
    Age 30-45
    Tech Proficiency Moderate. Uses Salesforce daily but complains about it. Excel power user.
    Decision-Making Role Power user. Provides input during CRM evaluation but executive/IT decides.
    CRM Experience Typically 5+ years using Salesforce, may have used Oracle or SAP.

    Goals

    • Manage complex multi-stakeholder deals (8-20 touchpoints) with clear visibility
    • Track POCs, evaluations, procurement cycles — not just simple pipeline stages
    • Collaborate with SEs, CS, and management on deal strategy within the CRM
    • Accurate forecasting for comp planning

    Pain Points

    • Salesforce is slow and bloated. Too many fields, too many clicks.
    • Multi-threaded deal tracking is manual — stakeholder maps live in spreadsheets.
    • Forecast call prep is painful — exporting data, reconciling with gut feel.
    • Can't easily see what the SE or CSM did with the account recently.
    • Territory changes break historical pipeline visibility.

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Review pipeline, check for new activity from SE/CS team members
    2. Qualification calls and discovery meetings
    3. Demo coordination with SE — handoffs need logging
    4. Proposal/negotiation stage — track pricing discussions, approvals
    5. Internal forecasting updates — manager reviews every deal
    6. Post-sales handoff to CS — ensure smooth transition

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Multi-threaded deal tracking — stakeholder maps with influence weights
    • Team activity feed — see what SE, CS, management did on each account without digging
    • AI forecasting — predictive close dates based on activity patterns, not manual entry
    • Fast performance — sub-second page loads, no waiting
    • Notes with AI summaries — meeting notes auto-summarized, action items extracted

    Persona 3: Morgan — Sales Manager

    Attribute Detail
    Role Sales Manager at 10-200 person company. Manages 5-15 reps.
    Age 30-45
    Tech Proficiency Moderate. Good with reporting tools. Not a builder/admin.
    Decision-Making Role Recommends tools, has budget input. Strong influence on CRM selection.
    CRM Experience Has used 1-2 CRMs. Mix of CRM experience and management instinct.

    Goals

    • See real-time pipeline health without hunting through spreadsheets
    • Identify deals at risk — coach reps before deals slip
    • Understand why deals are stalling — stage duration, activity gaps, stalled follow-ups
    • Run accurate weekly forecast calls without chasing reps for manual updates
    • Coach reps on specific deals — see what happened, where to focus

    Pain Points

    • Pipeline reports are always 2-3 days stale because reps don't update in real-time
    • CRMs surface raw data but not insights — she has to do the analysis herself
    • Can't easily see rep activity levels vs. results — who's working hard vs. who's coasting
    • Forecast calls devolve into "trust me" conversations without CRM data backing
    • No easy way to see coaching opportunities — which deals need intervention

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Pipeline dashboard — check stage distribution, deal velocity, at-risk deals
    2. Mid-day: Rep 1:1s — review specific deals, review activity metrics
    3. Review forecasts — adjust close dates, escalate at-risk deals
    4. Pipeline reviews with director — present team view, explain movement
    5. Coaching — identify process breakdowns, assign training
    6. End-of-month: Pipeline generation forecasting for next month

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Real-time pipeline dashboard — auto-updated from rep activity, no manual reporting
    • Deal velocity analysis — time-in-stage, activity recency, stalled deal alerts
    • Rep activity analytics — calls/emails per day, pipeline created, conversion rates
    • AI deal risk scoring — flags deals that are at risk of slipping before the rep knows
    • Manager dashboard — team-wide health + drill-down per rep, per deal
    • Forecast accuracy tracking — compare predicted vs. actual close dates

    Persona 4: Riley — Sales Director / VP of Sales

    Attribute Detail
    Role Sales Director or VP of Sales at 50-200 person company
    Age 35-55
    Tech Proficiency Low-medium. Uses email, calendar, dashboards. Doesn't want to click through menus.
    Decision-Making Role Primary buyer for sales tooling. Controls budget up to $50K/yr.
    CRM Experience Has seen 3-5 CRMs across multiple roles. Knows what works and what doesn't.

    Goals

    • Accurate revenue forecasting to the board/investors
    • Territory planning — ensure coverage without overlap or gaps
    • Identify systemic pipeline problems (top-of-funnel gap, long sales cycles, etc.)
    • Measure rep and team performance objectively
    • Scale the sales org — onboarding, process standardization, playbooks

    Pain Points

    • Forecasting is a black art — CRM data is always wrong, manager adjustments are manual
    • Territory disputes — reps fight over accounts, no single source of truth for assignment
    • No visibility into sales process adherence — reps skip stages, hide stalled deals
    • Onboarding new reps takes 4-8 weeks to CRM proficiency
    • Can't get reliable answers to "how did we perform last quarter" without analyst time
    • Buying CRM feels like a hostage situation — locked into contracts, hidden costs

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Executive dashboard — revenue snapshot, pipeline by stage, weekly trends
    2. Forecast review with managers — challenge assumptions, pressure-test numbers
    3. Strategic decisions — hiring plans, territory splits, comp adjustments
    4. Board/investor reporting — pipeline coverage ratio, win rate, ACV trends
    5. Sales process improvements — identify bottlenecks, update playbooks
    6. Vendor management — tool stack review, integration health

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Executive dashboard — single pane for revenue, pipeline coverage, forecast accuracy
    • Territory management — visual territory map with account assignment
    • Sales process analytics — funnel conversion rates, stage-by-stage win rates
    • Playbook and methodology support — embed sales methodology into the CRM
    • Multi-period forecasting — current quarter, next quarter, stretch goals
    • No hidden costs — predictable pricing that doesn't surprise the board

    Persona 5: Casey — CRM Admin / IT Manager

    Attribute Detail
    Role CRM Administrator or IT Manager at 50-500 person company
    Age 28-50
    Tech Proficiency Very high. May know Apex/Deluge scripting or be a "citizen developer" using Flow.
    Decision-Making Role Evaluates technical fit. Strong veto power — will block adoption of tools that can't integrate.
    CRM Experience Lives in the CRM daily. Configures fields, workflows, integrations, user permissions.

    Goals

    • Configure the CRM to match business processes without writing code
    • Maintain data quality — deduplication, import/export, field validation
    • Integrate CRM with the rest of the tech stack (ERP, billing, marketing tools)
    • Manage user access, roles, permissions cleanly
    • Train new users and maintain adoption

    Pain Points

    • Salesforce Apex/Deluge = proprietary lock-in. Every customization is a technical debt.
    • "Simple" changes (add a field, create a report) require release cycles and admin approval.
    • Data import/export is harder than it should be — CSV mapping, encoding problems.
    • Integrations break when the CRM updates (breaking changes, API deprecations).
    • Managing 200-500 users means provisioning/deprovisioning overhead.
    • CRM platform upgrades force retesting of all customizations.

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Check integration health, review error logs, verify data syncs
    2. User requests — add fields, modify picklists, create reports, set up new users
    3. Workflow/automation maintenance — troubleshoot failed flows, update automations
    4. Data management — deduplication runs, import leads, export reports
    5. Integration monitoring — email sync, ERP sync, marketing tool connections
    6. Release testing — verify customizations don't break on new CRM version
    7. Training — onboard new users, create documentation

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • No-code configuration — fields, picklists, page layouts, all drag-and-drop
    • API-first design — RESTful API with clear docs, GraphQL support, webhooks
    • Bulk import/export — clean CSV/Excel handling, mapping preview, conflict resolution
    • Role-based access control — granular permissions, teams, sharing rules
    • Backward-compatible releases — opt-in upgrades, never forced breaking changes
    • Integration marketplace — pre-built connectors, no-code webhook setup
    • Audit log — full activity history for compliance and troubleshooting

    Persona 6: Sam — Small Business Owner

    Attribute Detail
    Role Founder / Owner / CEO of a 3-15 person business (micro-SMB)
    Age 30-55
    Tech Proficiency Low-medium. Uses email, calendar, QuickBooks. Doesn't want to learn yet another tool.
    Decision-Making Role Sole decision-maker. Signs the check.
    CRM Experience May have tried a free CRM, given up, and gone back to spreadsheets.

    Goals

    • See all customer interactions in one place — know what's been promised, what's due
    • Not miss follow-ups — automated reminders for overdue tasks and stalled leads
    • Minimal setup time — should be useful on day 1, not after a week of configuration
    • One predictable monthly cost — no surprise add-ons or tier upgrades
    • Focus on running the business, not adminning a CRM

    Pain Points

    • Spreadsheet CRM doesn't scale but real CRMs are too complex/time-consuming
    • Can't afford a CRM consultant or dedicated admin
    • HubSpot's free tier is limited; paid tiers are expensive for a small team
    • Doesn't have time to train employees on a complex system
    • Needs sales AND support AND invoicing visibility but doesn't want three separate tools
    • Data lock-in fear — investing hours to set up a CRM then can't easily leave

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning scan: New leads, open support tickets, pending follow-ups
    2. Customer outreach: Calls, emails — needs quick context on last interaction
    3. Review pipeline: Which deals can I close this week?
    4. Customer support: Quick ticket resolution, assign to team member
    5. Billing follow-up: Check who hasn't paid, send reminder
    6. Evening: Review what happened today, plan tomorrow

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Minimal setup — import contacts from Google Contacts, start using in 10 minutes
    • Simple what-you-see dashboard — leads, deals, tickets, tasks in one view
    • All-in-one — sales + support + basic billing in one product
    • Flat pricing — one price for the whole company, all features included
    • Email + calendar sync — out of the box, no configuration
    • Quick data export — feels safe to commit because leaving is easy
    • Mobile-first — runs the whole business from a phone

    Persona 7: Taylor — Customer Support Agent

    Attribute Detail
    Role Customer Support / Success Agent at 50-500 person company
    Age 22-40
    Tech Proficiency Moderate. Comfortable with ticketing systems, chat, email.
    Decision-Making Role End user. Provides feedback but does not select tooling.
    CRM Experience Uses the support module of the CRM (Service Cloud, HubSpot Service Hub, Zendesk).

    Goals

    • See full customer history — sales context, past tickets, open deals — when a ticket comes in
    • Resolve issues quickly — find answers in the CRM without searching multiple systems
    • Log interactions without disrupting workflow — ticket updates, call notes, email replies
    • Escalate to sales when a support issue has revenue implications
    • Track SLAs and response times for performance reviews

    Pain Points

    • Support and sales data are siloed in different systems
    • Have to ask "what's the deal status" — no visibility into whether the customer is in procurement
    • Can't see if the customer just got upsold before a support call (changes the tone)
    • Ticket routing ignores relationship history — gets assigned to wrong person
    • No centralized knowledge base integrated with the CRM
    • Escalating a sales-related issue means leaving the CRM and sending a Slack message

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Check ticket queue, review SLA deadlines, prioritize
    2. Respond to tickets/emails — needs customer 360 view for context
    3. Log calls — document issues, resolutions, customer sentiment
    4. Internal escalations — flag billing or sales-related issues
    5. Knowledge base updates — add common solutions for self-service
    6. End-of-day wrap: Close resolved tickets, update activity log

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Customer 360 — unified view of sales pipeline + support tickets + interaction history
    • Ticket management — queue, assignment, SLA tracking, status pipeline
    • Shared context with sales — see open deals, recent interactions, account health
    • Collision detection — notify if someone from sales is also talking to this customer
    • Knowledge base — integrated, searchable, with AI-suggested answers
    • Collaboration tools — internal notes, @mentions, ticket assignment

    Persona 8: Dana — C-Suite Executive (CEO / CRO / COO)

    Attribute Detail
    Role CEO, Chief Revenue Officer, or COO at 50-500 person company
    Age 40-65
    Tech Proficiency Low. Uses email, probably an iPhone. Does not interact with CRM directly unless forced.
    Decision-Making Role Ultimate buyer. Signs the contract. ROI-focused.
    CRM Experience Has been pitched by every major CRM vendor. Skeptical of "transformation" claims.

    Goals

    • Revenue predictability — know what next quarter looks like with confidence
    • Team productivity — is the sales team selling or adminning?
    • Customer retention — at-a-glance view of churn risk, customer health scores
    • Operational efficiency — reduce tool bloat, consolidate vendors
    • ROI on tool investments — is the CRM paying for itself?

    Pain Points

    • Has 5+ different dashboards (CRM, BI tool, spreadsheets) and none of them agree
    • Forecasts are consistently wrong — always surprised by misses
    • No clear view of team performance — who's carrying the load, who's underperforming
    • CRM vendor relationships are adversarial — locked into contracts, hidden price increases
    • Can't get a straight answer on tool consolidation — every department has their own system
    • Customer churn is visible only after the fact — no early warning system

    Daily Workflow

    1. Morning: Executive dashboard — ARR, pipeline coverage, churn, cash flow
    2. Leadership meetings: Revenue review, strategy, resource allocation
    3. External: Board calls, investor updates, customer meetings
    4. Decision-making: Product direction, hiring plans, partnership decisions
    5. Review: Key deals in flight, large customer health, competitive threats
    6. No direct CRM interaction — gets data from VP of Sales or BI team

    What They Need From FrontierCRM

    • Executive summary dashboard — ARR, pipeline coverage ratio, NRR, churn rate
    • Customer health scoring — AI-driven risk flags before churn
    • Revenue forecasting — roll-up from team forecasts with confidence intervals
    • Team performance KPIs — quota attainment, ramp time, rep productivity
    • Vendor consolidation — sales + support + basic billing in one platform
    • Transparent reporting — no black-box forecasts, data they can trust
    • Predictable total cost — no "we outgrew our tier" surprises

    PART 4: PERSONA COVERAGE & CONSOLIDATION ANALYSIS

    Persona Clusters

    The 8 personas naturally cluster into 4 functional groups that share feature needs:

    Cluster Personas Shared P0 Features Design Implication
    Frontline Sellers Alex (SMB Rep), Jordan (Ent Rep), Sam (Biz Owner) Pipeline, Email sync, Auto-logging, Mobile, AI next-best-action Fast, low-click UI. Mobile parity. Zero data entry friction.
    Sales Leadership Morgan (Mgr), Riley (Dir/VP), Dana (Exec) Dashboards, Forecasting, Activity analytics, Deal velocity Insight-forward design. Dashboards, not data entry. Executive summary layer.
    Operations & Admin Casey (Admin) No-code config, API, RBAC, Import/export, Audit, Integration Power tools. Not default UI — accessed via settings/workspace. Backward compatibility.
    Customer Facing Taylor (Support), Sam (Biz Owner as support handler) Ticket management, Customer 360, Knowledge base, SLA Unified sales + support view. Collision detection.

    Key Consolidation Decisions

    Based on cross-persona analysis:

    1. Email sync is the single highest-ROI engineering investment. Every selling persona rates it P0. Salesforce's weakness here is FrontierCRM's wedge opportunity.

    2. Mobile is not optional — it's a fundamental requirement. 5 of 8 personas rate it P0. Must deliver full feature parity, not a trimmed-down companion.

    3. AI must be included, not upsold. Lead scoring, next-best-action, meeting summaries, and email drafting all score 12+. Gating these behind premium tiers contradicts the transparent-positioning strategy from T1.

    4. Support/Service features should ship in the same product, not as a separate SKU. The all-in-one positioning is a market gap. Taylor and Sam both need customer 360 with ticket management.

    5. Admin tools should be powerful but invisible. Casey (Admin) has P0 needs that no other persona cares about. Build a dedicated admin workspace rather than cluttering the main UI.

    6. Territory management and comp planning are Phase 2. Only Riley and Dana need these. Don't build before core pipeline and reporting are solid.

    7. Backward-compatible releases are non-negotiable for admin adoption. Casey will block adoption if forced migrations are a concern. This is FrontierCRM's answer to Salesforce's #3 user complaint — forced feature retirement and breaking changes.
